Before we start —
here's exactly what TAB sees.
TAB is your execution layer — not a monitor, not a judge. Before it does anything, you decide what it can observe. This is a real choice, not a formality.
Morning briefing required
TAB reads your calendar and open tasks to prepare your day. No content is stored — only structure.
Friction detection
TAB notices when you've been stuck — long doc, idle time, tab switching. Patterns only, no content.
Anonymous org contribution
Aggregated patterns (never you individually) flow to your org's infrastructure dashboard. Minimum k=5 anonymity.
Intervention feedback
When you rate a bridge (helpful / not), that signal trains TAB for your patterns. On-device only.
You can pause TAB entirely at any time — one tap, no explanation needed, nothing flagged to your manager.

The questions that matter
If we can't answer these, we shouldn't build this.
Every accelerator, every investor, every design partner will ask. Here's what we tell them — with data.
How do you prevent this from becoming surveillance?
↓
"Anonymous by architecture, not by policy. The server never receives identifiable data — it's stripped on-device before anything leaves. You can't surveil what was never collected."
Technical guarantee
Zero-knowledge encryption. Personal TAB holds keys. Management TAB receives only k-anonymized aggregates (k≥5).
Employee control
One-tap pause, no explanation needed. TAB stops observing instantly. Org dashboard shows "1 paused" but never who.
Proof point
Microsoft Viva built on named data — can't pivot without contradicting existing products. We're anonymous from day 1.
Why would employees actually adopt this?
↓
"Because it saves them 38 minutes a day — and they feel it immediately. Not next quarter. Today."
Day 1 value
Morning brief = 5 saved clicks. Background handling = 6 tasks off their plate. Friction detection = unstuck in 90 seconds instead of 12 minutes.
Trust gradient
Week 1: TAB suggests, confirms everything. Week 4: TAB acts, notifies after. Week 12: Silent execution, transparency strip.
Pilot signal
Tel Aviv school pilot: 47 of 65 teachers opted in voluntarily after seeing one demo. Veterinary clinic: 100% adoption within 48 hours.
What stops management from misusing the data?
↓
"The data they receive literally can't identify individuals. It's statistically impossible with k-anonymity and Byzantine fault tolerance."
Byzantine consensus
Observer TAB cross-checks all Personal TABs. One corrupted agent can't poison the system. Minimum 5 employees per metric.
What management sees
"Claims dept has 8.2 friction score." Never: "Sarah from Claims is struggling." The architecture makes individual targeting impossible.
Audit trail
Every org dashboard query is logged. Employees can see "Management pulled Claims data 3x this week" — full transparency both ways.
Why schools first? Why not start with tech companies?
↓
"If it works in schools — the hardest environment — it works everywhere. And we have warm pipelines ready to activate."
Why schools are hard
Non-technical staff. Infinite tool variety. Zero budget for onboarding. Emotional labor not just cognitive. Burnout = student outcomes suffer.
Current pilots
Tel Aviv elementary (721 students, 65 teachers). Veterinary clinic network (7 locations, 40 staff). Both halted when Iran attacked, pipelines warm.
Tech companies after
Once we prove it works for teachers managing 30 kids + infinite admin — selling to Salesforce is easy mode.
What's your unfair advantage against Microsoft, Google, etc.?
↓
"They can't pivot to anonymous architecture without contradicting their existing products. We're built on zero-knowledge from day one."
Microsoft Viva
Built on named employee data. Productivity scores tied to identities. Can't anonymize without killing core features. Market backlash in 2020 still remembered.
Google Workspace
Admin console shows who's active when. Zero anonymization layer. Cultural DNA = data collection for ads, not privacy-first infrastructure.
Our bet
In 5 years, employee-protective AI infrastructure = regulatory requirement in EU. We're compliant today. They'll need 3 years to rebuild.
How do you make money if employees can pause anytime?
↓
"Organizations pay per seat, not per active user. They're buying infrastructure — like Slack or email — that's available whether you use it that day or not."
Pricing model
€8/seat/month (1–50 seats), €6/seat (51–250), €4/seat (251+). Org pays monthly whether employee pauses or not. We're infrastructure, not SaaS usage.
Why orgs renew
Anonymous friction dashboard = system diagnostics they never had. Even if 20% pause daily, org still sees where infrastructure breaks. That data alone = renewal.
Retention signal
Veterinary pilot: 0 pauses in first week. Teachers: 12% paused during parent-teacher week (stressful), all resumed after. Voluntary pause ≠ churn risk.
